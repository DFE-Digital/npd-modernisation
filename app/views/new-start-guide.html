How to apply for access to NPD data

Published 25 September 2018
Last updated 5 February 2019 — see all updates
From: Department for Education

There's currently a 3 month lead time on processing applications

Contents

  1. Prepare to apply
  2. Find out what information is available in the NPD
  3. New or existing applications?

  5. Application approved

We are working with the Office for National Statistics (ONS) to enable access to de-identified individual level data for research.

For most applications, Department for Education (DfE) data will now be provided though the ONS Secure Research Service.

Access to the Secure Research Service may not be suitable if your request meets one or more of the following conditions:

  • Data is being processed to fulfil an essential public task (the data used is essential to run education and children’s services)
  • You’re doing research funded by, commissioned by, or contracted to the DfE or other government departments and the Secure Research Service is not suitable
  • You have approval from DfE before 1 June 2018, with agreement you would receive an extract, and the Secure Research Service is not suitable
  • You want to re-use data already held by your requesting organisation and within the existing licence period

The application process

Prepare to apply

You'll need to complete these steps before you can have access to the data.

  1. Get a basic DBS check
  2. Register as an accredited researcher with the ONS
  3. Apply for access to the ONS Secure Research Service

Find out what information is available in the NPD

You can use this to find out more about the types of data held within the NPD.

  • Find variables and metadata

New or existing applications?

If you have an application in process and have any questions, please email us at data.sharing@education.gov.uk and we’ll get back to you as soon as possible.

When you are ready to make an application, please contact data.sharing@education.gov.uk and we’ll:

  • send you an application form and guidance to complete it – including the new method for classifying data identification risk and sensitivity levels
  • help you through the Data Sharing Approval Panel (DSAP) approval process
  • provide the final agreement and declaration for you to sign

Application review

Your application will need to be reviewed by the DSAP panel before a decision can be made.

What is DSAP?

The Data Sharing Approval Panel (DSAP) is a Departmental group consisting of members of staff from across the Department. DSAP supports the Department in fulfilling its duties by reviewing and deciding whether to approve applications for access to personal identifiable data.

Application approved

Application approved?

You'll be sent an email detailing what to do next.

Accessing the data using the Secure Research Service

Once you are an ONS approved researcher and your data application has been approved, there are 2 ways you can access DfE data for approved projects through the Secure Research Service. You can use:

  • Physical labs – you can book a session to access DfE data at 1 of 5 ONS locations across the UK
  • Remote access – once your organisation meets security standards and have organised connectivity with ONS, you can access data online using your own equipment subject to specified conditions

For more details on how to become approved for remote access, please contact research.support@ons.gov.uk

Application denied?

If your application has been denied then you will be contacted to discuss the reasons why.

If you have any questions or queries regarding the data or the process of applying please get in touch with us at data.sharing@education.gov.uk
