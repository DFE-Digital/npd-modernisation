Research Project
Application


Accredited Researchers who require access to unpublished data, protected under the SRSA (2007), will need to submit a Research Project Application.

Your application must contain the following information;

  • A summary of your research proposal
  • An explanation of how this project would serve “the public good”
  • The data required for analysis
  • The names of all Accredited Researchers who will work on the project
  • An estimate of the time required for the project to be completed
  • Details of how and where the results of the project will be published upon completion
Does your project serve the public good?

The primary purpose of all Research projects must be to serve the public good in one or more of the following ways:

  • To provide an evidence base for public policy decision-making
  • To provide an evidence base for public service delivery
  • To provide an evidence base for decisions which are likely to significantly benefit the UK economy, society or quality of life of people in the UK
  • To replicate, validate or challenge Official Statistics
  • To replicate, validate or challenge existing research
  • To significantly extend understanding of social or economic trends or events by improving knowledge or challenging widely accepted analyses
  • To improve the quality, coverage or presentation of existing statistical information
